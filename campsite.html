Culy Hill Campsite

4 acres and while surprisingly close to the town of Ilkley it is a true country hideaway and a haven of adventure

Booking Information

About

The Scout Centre at Curly Hill is situated on the wooded slopes of the Wharfe Valley overlooking Ilkley Moor and the famous Cow & Calf Rocks. The Campsite occupies around 4 acres and while surprisingly close to the town of Ilkley it is a true country hideaway and a haven of adventure and fun for all ages, with the potential to see wildlife at first hand. It contains several discrete and separate camping grounds situated within the woods.

Capsite Details

The space in front of the building is generally kept clear for sports, games and other activities. Bookings can be taken from multiple groups but total numbers are limited to prevent overcrowding.
Your Duty Warden will endeavour to match your preference for a particular site but this will depend on ground conditions at the time of your visit. Should it be desired, it is possible to book exclusive use of the whole Site, including the Centre– see ‘Fees’ for more detail.
The maximum number of campers is set at 75.

Disabled Visitor

Consideration has been given to accommodating disabled visitors as far as possible however the site does include some steep slopes and rough ground which could cause difficulty for wheelchair users or people with mobility issues. Campsite facilities include an accessable 'wet-room' with disabled friendly washbasin, toilet and shower.

Facailites Summary

Amenities Block: There is a modern heated Amenities Block, providing toilets, hot and cold washing and shower (x4) facilities for both male and female campers. The block also contains a DDA compliant wet-room, providing full washing, shower and toilet facilities for a disabled person.
Pot Wash: A covered area with sinks and hot & cold water is provided alongside the Amenities Block.
Wood Fires: Altar fires are on each site for open fires. Depending on availability a stock of wood may be at hand though visitors may wish to bring their own supplies. The camp-site has a camp fire circle, which doubles for that quiet period of reflection or as a centre for group meetings.
Cooking Stoves All sites have level hard standing for use with cooking stoves.
Water As well as the Amenities Block there are water points situated at Field, Scott & Sunrise sites

In 2019-2020, serveral improvements were made, includeing new toilet facilities improved paths and drainage and new seating for campfire circle. The Telegraph & Argus

Site Rules

A Duty Warden is on call for your stay - but not necessarily on site - we like you to feel ‘free’ to enjoy your stay.
The rules of the Centre are simple: leave the Campsite as you would wish to find it. Please - No grease pits or fire areas to be dug. Keep disposable BBQs off grassed areas.
Noise: Please be aware that we do have some close neighbours and we would be grateful if noise could be kept to an absolute minimum between 23:00 and 07:00 hours during your stay.

Ready to book?

Our booking page has availability, fees, and a contact form to request your booking.
We operate a policy of single occupancy so visitors have exclusive use of the whole building for their stay. The Campsite operates with its own facilities and is hired separately but for an additional fee visitors may wish to book exclusive use of the full site – see ‘fees’ for full details.

Customer Reviews

We have groups from all over the world enjoy Curly Hill. Read some below

Ilkley based childminders
Jo Thompson

I am part of a group of Ilkley based childminders who use Curly hill scout hut most weeks. We meet in the morning and spend most of the day using the facilities at the scout hut. We find there are many great benefits to using Curly hill. For the children it provides them with the chance to socialise in a larger group of children. Its close proximity to Middleton woods gives the children the chance to explore, learn and play outdoors. Curly hill scout hut is a great addition to our weekly activities and both the children and childminders have a great time there, our group has always had a warm welcome from the team of wardens. I cannot express enough what a great place it is for the local community and visitors alike.

Guiseley School
Alex Fradley

We have used Curly Hill twice now with 20-40 year 9 students from Guiseley School (and we are booked in again for June next year). I can say on both occasions that we were warmly welcomed and the site is perfect for our needs. We use the site as part of our assessed bronze DofE expedition and fits perfectly between Guiseley School and Bolton Abbey. It is a great site and nice and quiet. It is also handy that, even as a school group, we are able to let the students off and exploring knowing that they'll never be too far away.

About Us

The Curly Hill Centre provides hostel accommodation plus separate camping facilities for use by members and affiliates of the Scout & Guide Movements plus other youth organisations, charity and schools. It is owned by the District and managed on its behalf by a team of volunteer wardens.

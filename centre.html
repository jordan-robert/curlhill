Curly Hill Centre

A modern 43 bed hostel with a kitchen, showers and a large main hall

Site Map and Building Layout

About

The Centre contains a fully equipped kitchen, with a fridge, fridge/freezer, dishwasher, water boiler, seven burner cooker, grill and double oven, 2x microwaves, 2x toasters, along with a larder section with plenty of cupboard space & shelving. Crockery, cutlery and cooking utensils are all provided. Fold away tables can convert the Main Hall into a large communAL dining area. All rooms are centrally heated, hot water being provided by a high capacity gas boiler. There are male & female toilets, washing and shower facilities. All bunks have foam mattresses with fire retardant covers..

Safety

The Centre is equipt with fire extinguishers and smoke detectors/fire alarm which have been installed following the advice of local fire authorities and are subject to routine testing and inspection. Copies of the H&S policy and latest Safety Risk Assessments are available upon request from the Booking Secretary.


Disabled Visitor

Consideration has been given to accommodating disabled visitors, particularly wheelchair users. It is a single storey building with ramped access and all doors are easily negotiable and light switches at a convenient height. There is a 'wet-room' with disabled friendly washbasin, toilet and shower with shower seat. There is a separate bedroom designed for use by a disabled person and their carer.

About Us

The Curly Hill Centre provides hostel accommodation plus separate camping facilities for use by members and affiliates of the Scout & Guide Movements plus other youth organisations, charities and schools. It is owned by the Scout District and managed on its behalf by a team of volunteer wardens.
